A Bit About us

Welcome To Cherry Tree Hives

We are an exciting NEW business eager to teach the world about one of the world's oldest hobbies - Beekeeping! Located in the beautiful Welsh countryside of Powys, we offer an amazing opportunity to receive a "hands on" educational experience, located near the famous book-town, Hay-On-Wye.

Who We Are

Once this business was the idea of a young beekeeper. Now, it's his job!

"I always wanted to be self-employed and have a job that I absolutely love, and now I actually have both!

Beekeeping is such an exciting and inspirational hobby - I cannot describe how important honeybees are for the world and how incredible the different cycles they have are. It's just phenomenal and I am happy to be(e) sharing what I've learnt with people"

The Honey

Try our Honey package – it’s a great introduction to the workings of a Beehive and the equipment a Beekeeper needs to run an apiary! Turn, get briefed, and get hands on with the bees! You’ll be shown the different tools used, parts of a beehive, the life cycles of honeybees, and of course the bees themselves. And if you’re lucky, you might be able to spot the queen! Suitable for 1-2 people. Tea and Coffee provided. 1 hour session.

The Hive

Our Hive package is PERFECT for a group of 4! Children up to 12 years of age go FREE! This fun activity with honeybees is great for both parents and children, as we bring the bees right to the kid’s fingertips. This session includes an introduction to the equipment a Beekeeper needs, the different parts of a beehive, the life cycles of honeybees, and of course the hands-on bit with the bees where you’ll be able to identify all the different things in the hive! Suitable for up to 4 people. Tea and Coffee provided. 30 mins -1 hour session.

The Queen

The Queen package offers a more “nitty, gritty” experience. Aimed at those who are looking to take up the hobby themselves, this package is for YOU! You will be shown what is included in our Honey package, PLUS what to look for in a Bee Inspection, how to manage and control swarming, and how to perform different methods of beekeeping when problems arise! Suitable for 1-3 people. Tea and Coffee provided. 2 hour session.

IMPORTANT!

All experiences ARE WEATHER DEPENDANT! If the weather takes a turn for the worst on your selected day, we will either re-schedule or refund you.

FAQs

When does booking open/close?

The season for experiences beings from mid-April and ends in mid-September - weather dependant.

Are the sessions weather dependant?

YES! Please be sure to check your emails on the day of your booking, as we may have to contact you to cancel your appointment due to poor weather - we cannot open hives when it's too cold or raining! We will re-schedule, or may have to refund you.

What's the age limit?

All ages are welcome, but we recommend that chuldren under 5 years of age should not be handling the bees. Remember thought, under 16s go FREE with our Family Package.

What should I bring on the day?

Please makes sure to bring with you suitable footwear for potentially muddy terrain - wellies are advised! Please make sure you have sufficient, suitable clothing for the weather on the day - DO NOT bring brightly coloured clothing.

Will I get stung?

We have to take precaution and warn you that you may well get stung on these experiences. We will ask you to sign a disclaimer on the day you arrive to warn you that we do not accept the liability of injury in the unlikely event of you getting stung for any reason. Any questions, please feel free to ring us!

Is there local accommodation?

Yes! Here at the same address we offer a stay in the beautiful @littlemountainlamping converted train carriage! To book for a stay at a discounted Beekeeping experience, simply go to www.littlemountainglamping.co.uk!

Where are we?

We are situated. 20 minutes from the town of Hay-On-Wye, Wales. The address is: Cherry Tree Barn, Newchurch, Kington, Powys, HR5 3QF. (Note: Please do not follow, Sat-Nav when you have entered the village, Please ring: 01544 370799)

Can I buy Honey?

Honey is available to purchase once extraction and bottling is completed in August. The Beekeeper will update those who desire a jar with more information.
